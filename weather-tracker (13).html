The 6-factor scoring system · max 100 pts
📐
F1 · Divergence 35 pts max
The core signal — how far your model is from the market

The engine calculates modelHi − kalshiImpliedTemp. The bigger the gap, the higher the score — up to 35pts at ≥6°F divergence. Below 1°F scores 0. Direction (YES if model warmer, NO if cooler) is set here.

≥6°F → 35pts 4–5°F → 25pts 2–3°F → 15pts <1°F → 0pts
📈
F2 · Historical accuracy 20 pts max
Has your model beaten the market on recent days?

Looks at the last 10 logged history records for this city. Counts how many times your model had lower error than Kalshi. The more your model has beaten the market recently, the higher this scores. No history = 10pts (neutral).

8–10 wins → 20pts 5–7 wins → 13pts 3–4 wins → 7pts <3 wins → 2pts
🔁
F3 · Model consistency 15 pts max
Is today's divergence direction stable day-over-day?

Checks the last 3 history records. If the model was consistently warmer or cooler than the market in the same direction as today's signal, it scores higher. A one-day fluke divergence scores low here — a consistent directional lean scores high.

3/3 same direction → 15pts 2/3 → 8pts 1/3 → 3pts
💰
F4 · Odds value 15 pts max
Is the market price still offering real value?

Checks the cost of the target bracket. A 30¢ contract scores higher than a 65¢ one — because if the market already agrees with you at 65¢, most of your edge is already priced in. Sweet spot is 25–55¢. Above 70¢ scores near 0.

25–45¢ → 15pts 45–55¢ → 10pts 55–70¢ → 5pts >70¢ → 0pts
🌀
F5 · Volatility penalty −15 pts max
Is today a high-uncertainty weather setup?

Reads the OWM weather description for today. Dangerous setups — front passage, thunderstorms, fog — get penalized because even a correct model prediction can be busted by chaotic local conditions. Clear sky days get no penalty.

front/thunder → −15pts rain/fog → −8pts clear → 0pts
🕐
F6 · Time of day 10 pts max
Are you running the engine at the right time?

The 12Z GFS run drops ~7am ET and the 00Z run drops ~7pm ET. Running the engine within a few hours of those drops means you're acting on the freshest model data before the market fully reprices. Off-hours score lower.

7–10am ET → 10pts 7–11pm ET → 10pts other hours → 4pts
Decision flow · how a trade gets placed
Step 1
Score all cities
Engine runs scoreSignal() for every city simultaneously using live OWM + Kalshi data.
Step 2
Threshold check
Total score compared to your threshold (default 62/100). Below threshold → no trade logged.
Step 3
Bracket selection
Finds the Kalshi bracket where the model's predicted high falls. That's the target bracket.
Step 4
Direction set
YES if model is warmer than market implied temp. NO if model is cooler. Never both.
Step 5
Dupe check
Already logged a trade for this city today? Skip. One trade per city per day maximum.
Step 6
Log trade
Trade written to paper log with stake, bracket, payout, confidence tier, and score.
Payout math · how profits are calculated
✅
If you win
Bracket resolves in your direction

Kalshi contracts pay $1.00 at settlement. If you bought YES at 35¢, your $10 stake buys $10 / $0.35 = 28.57 contracts. At $1.00 each that's $28.57 total return — a profit of +$18.57.

Formula: profit = (stake / cost) × 100 − stake

❌
If you lose
Bracket resolves against you

Contracts expire worthless. You lose your entire stake. No partial recovery. This is why the volatility penalty and threshold exist — to avoid trading on low-confidence days where a bust is likely.

Formula: pnl = −stake

⚖️
Break-even win rate
How often do you need to be right?

At a given cost, your break-even win rate is cost / 100. Buying YES at 35¢ means you need to win 35%+ of the time just to break even. At 20¢ you only need 20% — but the market is telling you it's unlikely.

Edge = your true win rate minus break-even rate.

City-specific factors the engine can't see
🗽
New York
Sea breeze · coastal exposure
  • Sea breeze off the Atlantic can drop highs 5–8°F in summer
  • OWM often misses the marine influence on hot days
  • Check wind direction — onshore flow busts warm forecasts
🌊
Chicago
Lake breeze · notorious model buster
  • Lake Michigan breeze is one of the most reliable model busters in the US
  • Can drop highs 5–10°F in summer when winds shift onshore
  • OWM runs warm on lake breeze days — this is a known edge
🌴
Miami
Sea breeze timing · afternoon storms
  • Sea breeze fires daily in summer, capping afternoon highs
  • If convection fires early, the high often comes before noon
  • Models frequently run warm — NO trades can be valuable in summer
🏔️
Denver
Upslope/downslope · elevation effects
  • Downslope chinook winds can push highs 15–20°F above forecast
  • Upslope flow brings clouds and cold — huge busts possible
  • Watch 850mb wind direction — it tells you more than surface obs
🌲
Seattle
Marine layer · burn-off timing
  • Marine layer burn-off is the key variable — does it clear before noon?
  • If it burns off early, highs can exceed forecast by 5–8°F
  • If it lingers, highs can come in 8–12°F below OWM
🤠
Austin
Cap strength · convective outflow
  • In summer, cap strength determines if storms fire and crash the high
  • A capped day under full sun often runs 3–5°F above OWM forecast
  • Early storm initiation can drop the high by 10°F+

Daily Log
Every time you hit "Run Engine", today's OWM forecast and Kalshi implied temp are logged for each city. Next morning the app auto-fetches the NWS settlement and computes errors. Your model = OWM unless you override it.
💡

Your routine: Hit "Run Engine" each morning (~8–10am). Optionally type your own temperature prediction in the "Your model" column before running. The next day, hit "Settle now" on yesterday's row (or it auto-settles on next run). After 5+ settled days, F3 in the signal engine uses this real data.
